# Safe Harbor

CLRTY supports good-faith security research that follows this policy.

## Good-Faith Research

Research is considered good faith when the researcher:

- Acts to improve CLRTY security and protect users.
- Reports vulnerabilities privately and promptly.
- Avoids data destruction, privacy violations, extortion, disruption, and persistence.
- Tests only systems, accounts, wallets, nodes, and data they own or are authorized to test.
- Stops testing immediately if sensitive data, credentials, funds, or production infrastructure are exposed.
- Gives CLRTY reasonable time to validate and remediate before public disclosure.

## CLRTY Position

For research that follows this policy, CLRTY will not intentionally initiate legal action for accidental, good-faith security testing against CLRTY-controlled systems to the extent permitted by law and CLRTY's authority over the affected system.

This safe harbor does not authorize:

- Attacks against third-party systems.
- Social engineering or phishing.
- Physical security attacks.
- Denial-of-service or resource exhaustion.
- Exfiltration, retention, sale, or public disclosure of sensitive data.
- Unauthorized fund movement or balance manipulation.
- Destructive testing or persistence.

## Coordinated Disclosure

Researchers should not publicly disclose details until CLRTY confirms remediation or agrees to a disclosure timeline. CLRTY will aim to credit researchers where appropriate, unless anonymity is requested or legal/compliance constraints apply.
